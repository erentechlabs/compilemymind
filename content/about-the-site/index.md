Compile My Mind publishes practical technical explanations and guides about cybersecurity, identity and access management, networking, IT fundamentals, Microsoft Azure, Microsoft Entra ID, cloud certifications, system administration, infrastructure, and developer or IT tools.

## How publishing works

The publishing system discovers topics, checks their relevance, collects primary sources, generates an article, validates important technical claims, checks originality and metadata, adds relevant internal links, and publishes only after every critical automated gate passes. No individual human author or reviewer is presented.

## How sources are selected

Official product documentation, standards organizations, government publications, official engineering material, release notes, and original project repositories are preferred. Candidate pages are checked for accessibility, relevance, freshness, title alignment, duplication, and domain trust before they can support an article.

## How content is checked and corrected

Generated articles are checked for duplicate topics, unsupported claims, invalid code, excessive source similarity, malformed metadata, unsafe commands, broken links, and structural problems. A failed draft is repaired and revalidated automatically. If it cannot pass within the retry limit, it remains unpublished and its failure reason is stored in internal logs.

Substantive corrections update the visible modified date and technical verification date. Formatting-only maintenance does not.
